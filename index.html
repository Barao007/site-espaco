About

  • 2008

    First space capsule

    NASA selected the company in December 2008 to be one of the companies providing commercial resupply services for the space station.

  • 2010 - 2012

    SpaceX and NASA

    The Dragon spacecraft made a successful maiden flight on December 8, 2010. Then, on May 22, 2012, Dragon, in its C208 version, was launched for an important test: an attempt to dock the spacecraft at the International Space Station . Dragon arrived at the station safely on May 25 of that year. It was the first time that a private spacecraft docked with the space station.

  • 2015 - 2018

    Reunible Falcon 9

    SpaceX announced the Falcon 9 Reusable Development Vehicle in 2012. The Falcon 9 Full Thrust and Falcon Heavy variants followed in 2015 and 2018, and are both launched from the John F. Kennedy Space Center, as well as Cape Canaveral and Vandenberg.

  • 2023

    Heavy starts operating

    Falcon Heavy launches now cost around US$90 million each, compared to the US$62 million charged for using the Falcon 9.

Falcon 9 is a reusable, two-stage rocket designed and manufactured by SpaceX for the reliable and safe transport of people and payloads into Earth orbit and beyond. Falcon 9 is the world’s first orbital class reusable rocket. Reusability allows SpaceX to refly the most expensive parts of the rocket, which in turn drives down the cost of space access.

Starship

SpaceX’s Starship spacecraft and Super Heavy rocket – collectively referred to as Starship – represent a fully reusable transportation system designed to carry both crew and cargo to Earth orbit, the Moon, Mars and beyond. Starship is the world’s most powerful launch vehicle ever developed, capable of carrying up to 150 metric tonnes fully reusable and 250 metric tonnes expendable.

Starlink

The service created by Elon Musk, founder of SpaceX, aims to offer fast and comprehensive internet via satellite. Starlink uses technologies that guarantee cost reduction for customers and greater coverage in service provision. Each satellite has four high-performance antennas. Acting in combination, signals are transmitted quickly and at reduced costs. The thousands of satellites that will be placed in orbit will form a broad, high-capacity network, reaching regions that current telecommunications operators do not yet reach. The Starlink satellites have a flat panel format, weigh 260 kg and will be at low altitudes of 550 km, so they can disintegrate in the atmosphere more quickly when they become disused. They have a single solar panel to power the device, which makes it easier for the satellites to use energy.
